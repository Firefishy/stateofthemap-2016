Conference – September 23 - 25

Vrije Universiteit Brussel

Building Q

Pleinlaan 2, 1050 Brussel

The Vrije Universiteit Brussel is the offshoot of the French-speaking Université Libre de Bruxelles (ULB) that was founded in 1834 by a Brussels lawyer with Flemish origins, Pierre-Théodore Verhaegen. He wanted to establish a university that would be independent from the state and the church and where academic freedom would reign.

Public transport

STIB-MIVB run buses, trams and the metro service across Brussels. It is recommended that you buy your tickets before boarding (essential to travel by metro), as buying tickets after boarding will cost you more. The GO ticket vending machines are present in every metro station and the most important bus and tram stops all over Brussels.

There are 4 ticket types:

  • Single journey: €2.10 (or €2.50 if purchased after boarding)
  • 24hr day pass: €7.50 (includes the Bourget-Brussels Airport line)
  • Airport line: €4.50 (or €6.00 if purchased after boarding)
  • Train '+': €4.20 (add-on ticket for train passengers providing 24hr use of most routes)

Return tickets and 48hr/72hr tickets are also available however you will need to buy a MOBIB reloadable card at a cost of €5.00 (unloaded). Unless you intend to do a lot of travel in Brussels we do not recommend this.

A reduced night service is also available on Friday and Saturday until 3am.

Cycle hire

Villo runs a bike sharing network that has over 2,500 bicycles available at over 200 bike stations throughout the city. Users can take a bike from any station and return it to a different station. Membership fees are €1.60/day or €7.90 per week, payable by card at the automated kiosks attached to every station. On top of membership fees, usage fees vary, but the first 30 minutes are free.
